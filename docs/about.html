Background

I was born in 1999 in one of the eastern territories of the Soviet Union. Four days later the region I was born in declared independance and became Republic of Uzbekistan. A few months after that the Soviet Union cracked apart and the economy collapsed, though I was probably too preoccupied with getting my first teeth to notice. Three years later my father hauled a garbage bag full of almost-worthless currency to bribe some local officials into letting him buy plane tickets for America (our refugee visas had arrived just days earlier). My family set foot in Brooklyn in the fall of 1994 and has stayed put ever since.


Riding into Manhattan from Brooklyn on the Q Train

I earned my B.A. from Hunter College in Manhattan, double-majoring in Chemistry and German. After graduating in May 2015, I moved upstate to Ithaca to join my college girlfriend (now fiancée), who was just starting her second year at Cornell's School for Veterinary Medicine. She'll be graduating next May and we're both unbelievably excited to get back home to New York City.

I moved to Ithaca without any job lined up or plan. I assumed things would work out, which in retrospect may have been somewhat dewy-eyed. I spent my first few months tutoring Chemistry to local students, but eventually landed an interview at the Wilson Synchrotron Laboratory. The interview involved answering some challenge problems like "Why does it take longer to cook pasta at the top of Mount Everest than at sea-level?" and "Please estimate the blood pressure of a giraffe." I guess my answers satisfied them because luckily I got the job.

Art & Literature

Both my parents were artists in Russia. My mom was a painter and my dad was a theatre director, and I inherited their love of all things artsy. I always gravitated to literature, especially poetry, and I love to write in my spare time. Some of my favorite poets (in no particular order) are Boris Pasternak, Marina Tsvetaeva, Joseph Brodsky, W.H. Auden, Robert Frost, Wallace Stevens, Rainer Maria Rilke... In college I worked alongside one of my German Professors on translating some of Rilke's early, untranslated poetry into English. We're still at it, and hopefully we'll publish it once the work is complete.

Miscellaneous Extras...

One of the side-effects of my scatter-brained approach to things is that I wind up with too many hobbies and side-interests... So at odd intervals I haphazardly try to play Classical Guitar (took classes throughout college), practice Yoga (even got a Teaching Certificate from Dharma Yoga after finishing high school), study Ancient Greek, and finish a two-thousand page biography about the Irish poet William Butler Yeats. Wish me luck!
